The Call Viewer — sidebar of calls, audio waveform, and synchronized transcript.
Steps
01 Pick a call from the sidebar on the left to load its audio and transcript.
02 Press Space to play or pause; ← → skip back or forward five seconds.
03 Click any line in the transcript to jump the audio to that exact moment in the conversation.
04 Use the search box at the top right to search within the current call’s transcript only.
05 Adjust playback speed (0.5× to 2×) for faster or slower review.
06 The active line highlights as audio plays, so you always know where you are in the conversation.

Section Five

Understanding AI Analysis

{{ case_name_short }}
transcripts/*.pdf

Every transcript PDF includes an AI-generated summary page right after the title page. It’s designed for quick triage — a starting point, not a substitute for listening to the audio.

Relevance Ratings
Relevance
High
Likely material to the case. Discussion of charges, co-defendants, witnesses, or potentially significant admissions.
Relevance
Medium
Potential context worth checking. Indirect references to the case, court dates, or legal proceedings that may add useful context.
Relevance
Low
Routine personal conversation. No apparent connection to case content. Safe to skip on the first review pass.
Summary Sections
Notes Timestamped, attorney-relevant moments only. Each cue carries the audio timestamp and the transcript page:line cite. NOTES: NONE means there’s nothing the model thought you’d need to flag.
Identity of Outside Party Who the inmate is speaking with and their apparent relationship — populated only when the call itself supports the inference.
Brief Summary A one or two sentence narrative overview of the call’s content and tone.

Important Notes

{{ case_name_short }}
disclaimer · review tips
Disclaimer
Read Before Use
Verify against the source recording before quoting or filing.
This package is generated using automated speech recognition and AI analysis. Transcripts may contain errors with names, technical terms, slang, or overlapping speech. Relevance ratings and summaries are intended as a triage aid, not a definitive assessment. Always verify critical details by listening to the original audio recording before quotation, filing, or use in court.
Tips for Efficient Review
01 Start with the Excel index sorted by the Relevance column to prioritize the calls most likely to matter.
02 Use the search page to sweep for specific names, phrases, or topics across every call at once.
03 In the viewer, click any line in the transcript to jump the audio to that exact moment.
04 Viewer keyboard shortcuts: Space play/pause, ← → skip 5 seconds, ↑ ↓ change speed.
05 Everything works offline — no internet, no upload. Just open any HTML file in a modern browser.
